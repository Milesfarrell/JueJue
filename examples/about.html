Testimony #4

My name is Patsy Kainuku, I am of Tuhoe decent, I am a social worker and my current mahi is to provide supervision, mentoring and training. Julie is my current supervisor and also provided supervision for me several years ago while I was a student social worker completing my fourth year placement. One of the fundamental principles of our supervision relationship is to promote and protect the best interests of the people I work with. Julie facilitates dialogue in session that promotes safe and accountable practice and supports me to critically reflect. Julie encouraged me to continue to develop and gain knowledge and skills required to be an effective supervisor/ mentor and educator. As a Maori clinician, having a supervision relationship that enables me to freely and openly explore my strong Maori world view is integral to my development and growth. Julie is able to facilitate processes that align with Te Tiriti o Waitangi, so that I was able to contextualise my views and integrate my learning into my practice. Julie is honest, open minded and always professional. I value her motivation to support me to grow my practice and reach my fullest potential.
